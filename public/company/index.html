SIF Overall

The continuance of the SIF is a primary objective and should be taken into account in every decision. The SIF is a long-term project of the University and should not be thought of as a semester-long project. The continual growth of the SIF will reflect credit on both past and present members, in addition to the University. It is in the best interest of the University and its general student body for the SIF to continue to grow.

About Us

Welcome to the Eberhardt School of Business Student Investment Fund. The philosophies of the SIF are intended to guide the students as they move their way through the semesters and to give the students a backbone of support during economic downturns.



  • Independence and Objectivity

    Members of the SIF must take reasonable care to ensure their independence and objectivity in analysis and recommendations. All recommendations given by SIF members reflect their personal objective decisions and should only be influenced by the evidence gathered and outcomes reached through independent research. All SIF members and faculty are encouraged to assist other members with research and analysis, as long as member objectivity is not compromised.

  • Loyalty and Care

    Members and faculty have a duty of loyalty to the University and themselves to act with reasonable judgement and prudent care for the SIF and its supervising boards. Members must act for the benefit of the University, the SIF and its supervising boards, and must promote a positive image for the SIF that reflects the Fund's philosophy and objectives.

  • Learning and Growing

    The SIF is a learning environment and should be treated as such. All opinions and recommendations will be given proper respect and thought before any judgment is passed. The SIF is not a revenue source for the University. As such, any losses or gains will be scrutinized and causes will be extracted for learning purposes.
